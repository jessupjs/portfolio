Mambo! Hi! How do?*

Welcome to my modest portfolio, which I trust my future self is keeping up-to-date. I am a data-driven designer and developer who deeply believes that visualization tools can remarkably support information systems and scientific analyses. My work typically crosses several domains (some academic, others firmly in the real world), and I truly, -uly, -uly, love learning and working.

At the moment I work as the gratis Director of Design and Development for a start-up called Heritage Foods Africa, as an Intern with the Visual Computing Group at Harvard, as the head Teaching Fellow for two DCE Courses, and as a freelance dev for a handful of tourism companies. I am relatively new to a collection of fascinating fields, inc. visualization, HCI, data science, and enabling tech, but eager and ambitious to dive deep.

If time could be so neatly divided, so too can my story within in: before Africa**, and in the years of. My early career was spent first in Nigeria, then in Zambia and finally in Tanzania, where I worked on very different versions of design and development. I was indelibly charmed. Like a monkey to unplucked maize, I am drawn to any afri-centric project.

Otherwise I have a small family with all members 4-or-younger, either by actual age or by maturity level. Seriously, it's good to be silly sometimes! Please connect with me if you feel the same*** about any of the above.
